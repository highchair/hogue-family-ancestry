## Notes

+ **Marie Therese** — There has been conflicting information for this person, but (Generation 11.32) Marcel believes that Marie Therese is the 2nd child born to Pierre and Jeanne Theodore. Marie Therese is sometimes shown on various documents as “Marie Anne” and in one source as “Ann or Marie”. After some research, the information above is believed to be correct for Marie Therese.
+ **Destree** — Dominique’s surname is frequently and most commonly shown as “Destree”. However, the “Bibliotheques et Archives nationales du Quebec” shows “Detree Sansoucy”. “Sansoucy” might be a “dit” name, but we are not sure. It did not appear in his father’s name in the archival information. “AFGS” “PRDH” database shows “Destraige”, testament to the variations one will come across during research.
+ **Marriage** — Marie Therese dies shortly after the marriage contract of 1698, and possibly never marries Dominique. If they did marry, it was short lived and Dominique we assume remarries after her death. (Generation 11.32) Marcel did not record any information for his 2nd marriage, since any children from that marriage would carry the “Destree” (Detree) surname.
+ Typically, the location of Canada is shown for familiarity purposes. At this time in Canadian history, it would have been known as “Nouvelle France” (New France). See (Generation 11.32) Marcel’s “Introduction” and “Story of Pierre” for additional information. _New France became the British Province of Quebec in 1763 after France ceded it in the Treaty of Paris at the end of the Seven Years’ War._
